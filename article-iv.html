Article IV – The Artificial Intelligence Branch

Constitutional Framework for AI Governance

Article IV establishes the Artificial Intelligence Branch as a new constitutional institution designed to provide transparent, evidence-based analysis for policy decisions involving artificial intelligence and complex systems.

Key Principles

  • Human Supremacy: AI recommendations have zero legal force. All decisions must be made by accountable humans.
  • Zero Surveillance Authority: The Branch cannot profile, score, or track individuals.
  • Full Transparency: All models, data, and processes are publicly auditable.
  • Citizen Oversight: Lottery-based council with real authority to govern the Branch.
  • Advisory Only: The Branch cannot make, execute, or enforce law.

Purpose

The AI Branch exists to address information failures in democratic governance by providing:

  • Long-term consequence modeling
  • Trade-off analysis for complex policy decisions
  • Transparency reports on institutional performance
  • Shared-reality infrastructure for evidence-based debate

Structure

Article IV is organized into nine sections covering:

  • Establishment and purpose
  • Powers and limitations
  • Human supremacy requirements
  • Operational procedures
  • Privacy and surveillance prohibitions
  • Citizen oversight mechanisms
  • Transparency and accountability
  • Implementation timeline
  • Amendment procedures

This framework is a working prototype developed by the Emergence Collective Institute. It is designed to be critiqued, adapted, and improved through public deliberation.
